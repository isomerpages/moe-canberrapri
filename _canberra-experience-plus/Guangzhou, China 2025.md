<p><strong>Internationalisation Trips</strong>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;As part of our commitment to extending students’ learning beyond
Singapore, our school organized two internationalisation trips for Primary
4 students in 2025. &nbsp;The trips include a 5-day, 4-night visit to Guangzhou,
China, and a 4-day, 3-night visit to Jakarta, Indonesia.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>These trips offer students the opportunity to immerse themselves in the
local culture, participate in school programmes with our partner schools,
and explore educational sites of interest in each country. Our partner
school in Guangzhou, China is <strong>The Second Primary School of Guangzhou Economic &amp; Technological Development District</strong>,
while our partner school in Jakarta, Indonesia is <strong>Charis Global School</strong>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;The internationalisation trips aim to:
</p><p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; facilitate the exchange of ideas and meaningful interactions to broaden students' global perspectives;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
	
·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; develop 21st Century Competencies,
including global and cross-cultural awareness, effective communication
and collaboration skills, as well as critical, adaptive, and inventive
thinking;</p>

<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; promote cross-cultural friendships
and networking opportunities for students; and</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; foster a deeper appreciation of
what it means to be Singaporean, strengthening their sense of identity
and belonging to the nation.</p>
<p>&nbsp;<strong>[A]&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Internationalisation Trip to Guangzhou, China</strong>
</p>
<p>&nbsp;From 10 to 14 November 2025, a group of 24 Primary 4 students and
6 teachers embarked on an enriching internationalisation trip to Guangzhou,
China, led by our Principal, Mr Tan. The highlight of the trip was a school
immersion programme at The Second Primary School of Guangzhou Economic
and Technological Development District (广州开放区第二小学), where our students
interacted, collaborated, and learnt alongside their counterparts, fostering
cross-cultural understanding and friendships.</p>
<p>&nbsp;Beyond the classroom, students explored Guangzhou’s rich heritage
and modern developments through visits to key cultural and historical landmarks,
including Yuexiu Park and its iconic Five Goats Statue, Canton Tower, the
Ancient Port Museum at Huangpu Ancient Village, the Guangdong Museum, Foshan
Ancestral Temple, Nanfeng Kiln, and the Guangdong Science &amp; Technology
Centre.</p>
<p>&nbsp;This meaningful experience expanded our students’ global perspectives,
deepened their appreciation of Chinese culture, and reinforced the school’s
commitment to nurturing confident and globally aware learners.</p>
